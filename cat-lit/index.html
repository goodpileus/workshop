Catlit

this is a personal collection of books (and stories in general) with cats in them. some of them i own, some i want to own, some i've read, and some i want to read. this is by no means a comprehensive list of all books including cats. the ones i have collected here are in some way significant or interesting to me.

? title author year
Master and Margarita Mikhail Bulgakov 1967

classic soviet novel. the devil has come to Moscow and with him is an entourage that includes the foolish Behemoth, a cat who speaks and walks upright.

Alice in Wonderland Lewis Carrol 1871
Engine Summer John Crowley 1979

ethereal book. one of my favorites of all time... set in a post-post-apocalyptic earth. includes a community of people called Dr. Boots List, who live with large cats and have modeled their society around the cats' demeanors and ways of being. they practice forgetting.

Not Wanted on the Voyage Timothy Findley 1984
Trim Matthew Flinders 1973

biography of Trim, a cat who was born aboard and lived on a ship. written by the ship's captain, Matthew Flinders, who loved Trim.



there is a monument to Trim in Sydney, Australia.

Millions of Cats Wanda Gág 1928
Feline Philosophy John Gray 2020

a bit too stuffy for a book about the philosophy of cats. lacks the sudden playfulness and wild energy of cats. there are some worthwhile illuminations, however, and the book has a nice cover illustration.

We Have Always Lived in the Castle Shirley Jackson 1962
If Cats Disappeared from the World Genki Kawamura 2012
Catwings Ursula K. Le Guin 1988
The Cat Office Kenji Miyazawa 1997
Once and Forever:
The Tales of Kenji Miyazawa Kenji Miyazawa 1994

collection of short stories. includes the story Wildcat and the Acorns.

the story Night on the Galactic Railroad was adapted into an animated film in 1965 by Gisaburō Sugii. the film depicts the characters as anthropomorphic cats. this is one of my favorite films.

an animated biopic about Kenji Miyazawa was also produced in 1996, titled Spring and Chaos and directed by Shōji Kawamori. the people are depicted as cats in this film as well.

Tarakabako wrote a post about Miyazawa in anime and manga here.

the novel begins with the search for the cat Noboru Wataya.
